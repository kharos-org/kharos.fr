The problem

OT devices are the blind spot in critical infrastructure security

Critical infrastructure operators run thousands of OT devices: IP cameras, badge readers, access control systems, intercoms. These devices come from multiple vendors, span multiple generations, and are rarely included in cybersecurity audits.

Teams manage them manually with spreadsheets and vendor-specific tools. Firmware versions are unknown. Certificate lifecycles are tracked in email threads. Configuration drift goes unnoticed until an audit flags it.

Meanwhile, security frameworks and compliance requirements are tightening. Regulators are no longer accepting "we'll get to it."

Real example: To update 802.1x certificates on 600 IP cameras, it took a dedicated team working several weeks. One brand at a time, one device at a time. This is not an edge case. This is the norm.
